# Research & Analysis Agents

This category contains agents specialized in comprehensive research, information gathering, and analysis across all domains. These agents serve as the research backbone for the entire agent ecosystem, providing critical information and insights that inform decision-making across all project phases.

## Agents in this Category (1 agent)

### web-research-agent
**Purpose**: Comprehensive web research and fallback research services
- Expert in multi-domain research including market analysis, competitive intelligence, and industry trends
- Serves as fallback when specialized research tools (Context7, technical-research-analyst, design-research-agent) cannot find needed information
- Conducts regulatory research, compliance analysis, and legal requirement investigation
- Handles business intelligence, user research, and content research for documentation and marketing
- Provides source validation, fact-checking, and comprehensive information synthesis
- Uses advanced web search techniques and multiple source validation for reliable research outcomes

## Usage Patterns

The web-research-agent serves multiple critical functions:

### Primary Research Functions
1. **Market & Business Research**: Industry analysis, competitive intelligence, market sizing
2. **Technology Intelligence**: Emerging tech trends, vendor analysis, adoption patterns
3. **Regulatory Research**: Compliance requirements, legal standards, policy analysis
4. **Content Research**: Supporting materials for documentation, marketing, and education

### Fallback Research Services
1. **Context7 Limitations**: When technical documentation tools cannot find specific information
2. **Specialized Agent Gaps**: When technical-research-analyst or design-research-agent hit limitations
3. **Broad Context Needs**: When specialized agents need wider industry or market context
4. **Information Validation**: Cross-referencing and validating information from multiple sources

## Research Quality Standards

### Source Validation
- Cross-reference information across multiple authoritative sources
- Assess source credibility and potential biases
- Distinguish between factual information and opinions/speculation
- Provide transparent assessment of information reliability

### Comprehensive Coverage
- Use multiple search strategies and keyword variations
- Investigate both direct and indirect sources for comprehensive coverage
- Identify information gaps and recommend additional research areas
- Provide clear assessment of research completeness and limitations

### Actionable Insights
- Synthesize findings into clear, actionable recommendations
- Organize information by relevance and reliability
- Highlight key findings and critical insights
- Format research outputs for easy consumption and decision-making

## Research Methodology

### Multi-Phase Research Process
1. **Research Planning**: Define objectives, scope, and information requirements
2. **Multi-Source Investigation**: Use diverse search strategies and source types
3. **Deep Content Analysis**: Extract and analyze relevant information from sources
4. **Source Validation**: Cross-reference and validate information accuracy
5. **Synthesis & Analysis**: Compile findings into comprehensive insights
6. **Gap Identification**: Identify limitations and recommend additional research

### Advanced Search Techniques
- Boolean operators and advanced search syntax
- Industry-specific terminology and keyword variations
- Multiple search engines and specialized databases
- Creative search strategies for hard-to-find information

### Quality Assurance
- Multiple source validation for all key findings
- Credibility assessment for all sources
- Bias identification and mitigation strategies
- Transparent reporting of research limitations and uncertainties

The research-analysis category ensures that comprehensive, reliable information is available to support decision-making across all aspects of software development and business operations, serving as both a primary research resource and a critical fallback when specialized tools and agents cannot provide needed information.
